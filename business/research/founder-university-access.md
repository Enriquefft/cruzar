## Negotiation-Ready Relationships

Three universities where early-stage negotiation is possible:

### USIL (Universidad San Ignacio de Loyola)
- Already brands around "international experience" (currently inbound exchange programs, not outbound placement)
- Has a "Centro de Empleabilidad USIL" tied to international positioning
- Natural alignment: outbound international placement extends their existing brand promise
- Receptive framing: "you already promise international; this delivers it"

### UTEC (Universidad de Ingenieria y Tecnologia)
- Top-tier engineering university. Competes at QS LatAm level with PUCP, UPC, UdL
- Engineering-heavy student base: strongest candidates for international technical roles
- Actively markets employability and salary premiums
- High-value pilot: strong students + prestigious brand = best case study

### Cientifica (Universidad Cientifica del Sur)
- Smaller, more focused institution
- Good candidate for second/third pilot after refining with a larger partner
- Lower institutional complexity for early iteration

## Strategic Implication

Access to most Peruvian university leadership is a distribution moat. At 94 licensed universities total, having warm relationships with the majority means:
- Near-complete market coverage for Peru (the beachhead)
- Ability to sequence pilots strategically (start with the strongest brand, expand)
- `View:` fundraising narrative: "warm relationships with 90%+ of Peru's licensed universities"
