## Direct Competitors (LatAm Talent)

### Mercor
- **$10B valuation**, $350M Series C (Oct 2025)
- NOT a recruiting company anymore. Actual business: supplying domain experts to AI labs for RLHF training data
- Run rate ~$840M gross revenue. Pays $1.5M/day to contractors. Customers: OpenAI, Anthropic
- "Recruiting" framing is legacy. This is an AI training labor marketplace.

### Revelo
- Very alive. Acquired 5+ LatAm talent companies in 30 months (Listopro in Mexico, Alto in Argentina)
- 400K+ developers. Clients: Intuit, Squarespace, Oracle. G2 Leader Fall 2025.
- The dominant LatAm dev marketplace. Employer-side only.

### Turing
- Active, AI-matched remote developers for US companies. No major new funding.
- Reviews cite cultural/communication mismatches. 50% below US rates.

### Terminal.io
- Active. Nearshore teams in LatAm, Canada, Europe. EOR + recruiting. 180 countries.

### Near
- Active. Positions as recruiting firm (not marketplace).
- Data: 84% of LatAm placements are mid/senior. BDR/SDR as top role. Not just engineering.

### Torre
- Operational but stalled. $15M total (seed-stage). 1M+ users. No new funding. No breakout traction.

### Athyna
- Small ($2.5M raised). Managed service. Launched "Athyna Intelligence" for AI post-training expert matching.

### Andela
- $1.5B unicorn (2021, no new round since). New CEO 2024.
- Acquired Woven (Jan 2026) for AI-native engineering assessment.
- Pivoted Africa-only to global.

## Adjacent Competitors (AI Career Tools)

### Prentus -- MOST RELEVANT
- AI career agents sold B2B to universities
- $435K ARR, 20 partner schools (DeVry, Miami Dade College)
- Mock interviews, resume builder, placement tracking
- **Domestic US only. No international component.**
- Proves the university B2B model works but is shallow on AI and geographically limited.

### Final Round AI
- Still operating. Mixed reviews, crashes during live interviews. B2C only. Not enterprise-grade.

### Sonara
- $24/month, auto-applies to jobs. Poor matching quality. Volume play.

### Interview Coder / Cluely
- Pivoted to real-time AI cheating for interviews/exams. $5.3M seed, $3M+ ARR.
- Cheating adoption doubled from 15% to 35% of candidates in H2 2025.

### Simplify
- Chrome extension, semi-automated form-filling. Free. Lightweight.

### LazyApply
- 2.1 stars Trustpilot. Broken automations, unreachable team.

### AIHawk
- Open-source, developer-only. High ban risk.

## Critical Findings

### 1. Nobody does university-to-international-job placement
Confirmed gap. Prentus proves university B2B works but is US-domestic only. Handshake dominates US career services, zero LatAm/international. LatAm marketplaces (Revelo, Near) sell to employers. No product makes LatAm university graduates internationally employable through the institution.

### 2. B2B to universities is a real but small category
Prentus, Ivy.ai, PathwayU, PeopleGrove. All US-domestic. None have diagnostic + voice training + placement stack.

### 3. Autonomous application is under regulatory siege
- California and Illinois: AI-in-hiring laws effective 2025-2026. Require human oversight, anti-discrimination audits.
- Federal "No Robot Bosses Act of 2025" pending.
- Employers flagging automated submissions in ATS systems, sharing flags cross-company.
- "Doom loop" (Greenhouse CEO): candidates 3x less likely to get hired than 3 years ago, recruiters process 2.7x more applications.
- 20% of employers considering "pay to apply" fees.
- **Building a core capability on an approach employers are actively trying to kill is a structural risk.**

### 4. "AI-native executive search" partially claimed
- Rocket (YC): "AI-native recruiting" for startups
- Spott: "AI-native ATS/CRM"
- HireGlide: "AI-native recruiting agency"
- None claim "abandoned middle" framing specifically
- Mercor has diverged from this space entirely (now RLHF labor)

## Positioning Implications

"AI-native executive search for the abandoned middle" has two problems:
1. Autonomous application leg is adversarial to employers the company needs
2. "Executive search" implies employer-side payment, but the product is sold to universities

**What IS differentiated and unclaimed:** AI-powered cross-border university-to-job placement, B2B to institutions. The university is the customer, the student outcome is the product, the employer relationship is a byproduct of quality.

**Suggested reframe:** "AI career infrastructure for universities" or "the institutional pipeline that turns LatAm students into internationally placeable talent."
