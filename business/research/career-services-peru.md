# Research: Career Services at Peruvian Universities

**Date:** 2026-04-13
**Sources:** University websites (PUCP, UPC, UdL, USIL, UNMSM, UNI), SUNEDU Informe Bienal. TODO sourcing: per-university structure descriptions, staffing counts, and US-comparison figures need URL + retrieval date per claim.

## Structure by University

- **PUCP:** "Bolsa de Trabajo PUCP" under alumni/employability office. One of the oldest in Peru.
- **UPC:** "Bolsa de Trabajo UPC" + "Centro de Empleabilidad" integrated with alumni network.
- **Universidad de Lima:** "Bolsa de Trabajo" under "Dirección de Educación Continua y Servicios al Egresado."
- **USIL:** "Centro de Empleabilidad USIL" tied to international positioning branding.
- **San Marcos (UNMSM):** "Bolsa de Trabajo UNMSM." Significantly less resourced than private counterparts.
- **UNI:** Basic "Bolsa de Trabajo" with limited scope.

## What They Do

Private universities: job board portals, annual job fairs ("Feria Laboral"), CV workshops, interview prep, company partnerships for internships ("prácticas pre-profesionales"), alumni networking events. PUCP and UPC have the most active employer partnership programs (BCP, Interbank, Belcorp, Alicorp, mining companies).

Public universities: basic job boards, occasional fairs. Lack consistent programming, dedicated staff, employer relationship management.

## Technology

Basic proprietary web portals. Filtered job listing pages, not matching/tracking systems. UPC uses a Salesforce-adjacent CRM for alumni tracking. None use Handshake, Symplicity, or other US-standard platforms. Technology gap is significant.

## Staffing

Private universities: ~3-8 staff in employability units.
Public universities: ~1-3 staff.
Compare US: 15-50+ full-time career services staff with dedicated counselors per college.

## International Placement

**Not on the radar as a systematic function.** USIL markets "international experience" but that's inbound (exchange programs), not outbound placement. Some PUCP graduates access international roles through personal networks or MBA pipelines, not institutional effort. No Peruvian university runs structured international job placement.

## Data/Standards

No standardized outcome tracking requirements for career services. SUNEDU collects graduate employment data but does not mandate career services standards. Public data on budgets, staffing, or KPIs for these offices is virtually nonexistent.

## Key Takeaway

Career services in Peru is a basic, under-resourced function focused on domestic internship/job listing. International placement doesn't exist as a category. The product isn't replacing or upgrading existing tools; it's introducing a capability that has no precedent in the Peruvian university system.
