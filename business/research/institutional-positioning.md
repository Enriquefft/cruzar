## What University Administrators Care About (ranked)

1. Placement rates (the single number accreditors and rankings demand)
2. Time-to-employment after graduation
3. Salary data for graduates (feeds rankings, justifies tuition)
4. Employer satisfaction / employer network size
5. Student engagement rates (proves ROI internally)

## Language That Resonates

- "Student outcomes" (universal currency)
- "Career readiness"
- "Real-world ready"
- "Data-driven" / "measurable"
- "At scale" / "without adding staff" (budget justification)
- "Personalized"
- "Streamline" (operational pain)

## Positioning Mistakes to Avoid

- Leading with technology ("AI-powered") instead of outcomes. Universities buy outcomes, not tools.
- Selling to the wrong person with the wrong pitch. Career services needs operational relief; Rector needs strategic differentiation.
- Ignoring accreditation/reporting angle. If the platform generates the data they already must report, that's a selling point.
- Consumer-facing language doesn't work for institutional buyers.

## Key Insight

Every competitor operates domestically (US students to US jobs, LatAm students to LatAm jobs). None place Latin American students into international remote jobs. "International" is the differentiator no one else can claim.

## Recommended Positioning

**"We place your students in international remote jobs and give you the data to prove it."**

Works for both audiences:
- Career services: "this solves placement, and I get a dashboard"
- Rector: "measurable outcomes I can report and market"
- Avoids leading with "AI"
- Concrete and falsifiable (builds trust)
- Asserts a capability no competitor claims
