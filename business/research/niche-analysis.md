## Existing LatAm Placement Competition by Niche

- **Software Dev:** Turing, Terminal, BairesDev, Toptal, Revelo, Torre, Andela, Coderslink, Nearsure, Encora. Red ocean.
- **SDR/BDR:** Somewhere (formerly SalesHire), Pronto Staff, NearSourcing, boutiques. No dominant player.
- **Customer Support:** SupportNinja, Boldr, CloudTask, Magic. Fragmented.
- **Data/Analytics:** No LatAm-specific player owns this.
- **Ops/PM:** Crowded at VA tier (Belay, Wishup). Underserved at PM/ops coordinator tier.

## Recommendation: SDR/BDR as Primary Niche

**Why SDR/BDR wins on all three criteria:**

1. **Large enough:** `Estimate:` $500M+ TAM, 20K-30K roles/year, growing 30%+ YoY. `Estimate:` US SaaS companies discovered LatAm SDRs deliver 60-70% of US SDR output at 25% cost. TODO sourcing.

2. **Soft skills are the hiring signal:** Communication, persistence, cultural fluency with US buyers. Tools (Outreach, Salesloft) are learnable in days. The hiring process is literally: mock cold call + roleplay + English screen + culture fit. 70% soft skills.

3. **Not crowded:** No dominant LatAm player. Boutique agencies exist but no AI-native placement company targeting this specifically.

**Why it compounds with Cruzar's advantages:**
- AI scenario simulation (cold call with realistic prospect) IS the job
- Soft skills validation is the entire evaluation
- CEFR B2+ is the English floor (not C2, broadens candidate pool)
- The failure mode for LatAm SDRs is communication/cultural fluency, which is exactly what the simulation validates
- Commission structures mean OTE $2.5K-5K/mo (still 3-5x local salary)

## Secondary Niches

- **Customer Support/Success (L2+ tier):** Huge volume, soft-skill-dependent, natural expansion from SDR
- **Ops/PM (non-VA tier):** Underserved, high soft-skill weight, harder to market as a category

RevOps, GTM operations, AI prompt engineering, compliance/regulatory for US companies.
