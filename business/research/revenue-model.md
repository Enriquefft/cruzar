## Key Finding

No one combines university-as-customer with outcomes-based placement fees. University SaaS is flat annual. Placement economics are always employer-funded. An anchor + outcomes model would be novel: de-risks the university buy while capturing placement upside that normally flows to staffing firms.

## Revenue Model Options

### Per-student SaaS license ($50-$500/student/year)
- Pros: Predictable, easy to budget, scales with enrollment
- Cons: Disconnected from outcomes, commoditizes toward $1/student floor
- Signals: "We are a software company"

### Per-cohort flat fee ($5K-$25K per cohort)
- Pros: Simple, predictable, natural pilot structure
- Cons: No outcome alignment, resistance if first cohort underperforms
- Signals: "We are a program provider"

### Per-placement success fee (15-30% of first-year comp or $1K-$5K flat)
- Pros: Zero risk for university, extreme outcome alignment, high revenue per placement
- Cons: Delayed/unpredictable cash flow, requires tracking/verification
- Signals: "We are a recruiting firm"

### Hybrid (anchor / platform fee + success fee)
- Pros: Base covers operating costs + signals university commitment, success fee captures upside, mirrors exec search retainer + completion
- Cons: Slightly complex to explain; bespoke per deal
- Signals: "We are a platform that delivers outcomes"

### Outcomes-based with milestones
- Pros: Most granular alignment, university sees value accruing before placement
- Cons: Most complex, milestone definitions become negotiation points
- Signals: "We are accountable at every step"

### Placement Fee from employer side (long-term)
- 10-15% of first-year salary per placed candidate
- At $30K avg placement: $3K-$4.5K per placement
- At $60K avg placement: $6K-$9K per placement
- Pro: aligns with placement value, scales with comp
- Con: requires employer relationship, longer sales cycle

### Two-sided (university + employer, long-term)
- University pays anchor/SaaS for the platform (diagnostic + dashboard + preparation)
- Company collects placement fee from employer side
- Two revenue streams from two sides of the market
- Pro: builds toward Handshake-like two-sided model at much higher margins
- Con: complexity

## Pilot Pricing Reference Points

`Estimate:` an $8K anchor + up to $12K outcomes for 40 students over 12 weeks (~$20K ceiling) sits within these reference points:
- $8K base is below the Handshake US benchmark (~$8K/year)
- $300/student if all 40 place is 0.5% of first-year comp at $60K, far below exec search 25-35%
- `Estimate:` $20K ceiling is within pilot budget range for mid-to-large Peruvian private universities ($10K-$30K typical for new initiatives)
- Risk for university limited to $8K if outcomes disappoint

`View:` significant room to raise success fees in future cohorts.
