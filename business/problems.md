# Problems & Pain

Pain measured on three axes:
- **Dolor** (emotional, reputational, career risk)
- **Tiempo** (time wasted, lost, or unavailable)
- **Dinero** (money lost, left on table, or at risk)

Scale: Low / Medium / High / Critical

---

## U1: The Student

| # | Problem | Dolor | Tiempo | Dinero |
|---|---------|-------|--------|--------|
| 1 | **No access to international hiring pipeline.** Doesn't know it exists, doesn't know how to enter, has no connections. The pipeline is invisible. | Critical | Critical | Critical |
| 2 | **Doesn't know what international roles exist for their profile.** Searches blind or doesn't search at all. Assumes local market is the only option. | Medium | High | High |
| 3 | **English level is uncertain or insufficient.** No honest, calibrated assessment. May be disqualified silently by employers without knowing why. | High | Medium | High |
| 4 | **Doesn't know their gaps or how to close them.** Wastes time on wrong preparation. Impostor syndrome without data. | High | High | Medium |
| 5 | **Can't practice real interviews.** Has never experienced a professional English interview for an international role. Fear and avoidance dominate. | High | Medium | Low direct, high indirect |
| 6 | **Defaults to local salary by inertia.** Earns USD 480-670/month when 3-5x is accessible. The delta is USD 12K-40K+/year left on the table. | Low (doesn't feel it yet) | N/A | Critical |

**The deepest pain:** #1 and #6 are the same problem from different angles. The student can't access a path they don't know exists. Everything else (English, gaps, interviews) is a preparation problem that only matters once access exists. The salary delta is life-changing (3-5x) but the student doesn't feel the pain because they don't know the alternative.

**Implication for the product:** The product must first make the opportunity visible (diagnostic: "here is what you could earn, here is what's in the way"), then make it accessible (training + placement). Awareness precedes preparation.

## U2: Career Services / Employability Office

| # | Problem | Dolor | Tiempo | Dinero |
|---|---------|-------|--------|--------|
| 1 | **No tools to assess student readiness at scale.** Everything is manual, per-student, or not done. | Medium | High | Low |
| 2 | **No visibility into student career pipeline.** Can't track who is progressing, stuck, or placed. Flying blind. | High | High | Low |
| 3 | **International placement isn't a capability they have.** Not that they're bad at it; they've never attempted it. No infrastructure, no process, no connections. | High | N/A | N/A |
| 4 | **Accountable for outcomes they can't produce.** Ponte en Carrera publishes graduate employment data publicly. Low outcomes hurt the university brand. Career services has no tools to move the numbers. | High | Medium | Medium |
| 5 | **Under-resourced.** 3-8 staff doing job fairs and maintaining a job board. Can't scale even domestic placement, let alone international. | Medium | Critical | Low |

**The deepest pain:** #3 and #4 together. They're implicitly responsible for graduate outcomes that are now publicly visible, but they have zero infrastructure for the highest-value outcome (international placement). The product doesn't upgrade their existing capability; it gives them a new one that didn't exist.

**Key tension:** U2 could be empowered by the product (finally able to deliver outcomes they're measured on) or threatened by it (an external tool doing what they should be doing, exposing their limitations). The product's relationship to U2 must be framed as amplification, not replacement.

## C1: VP Academic / Rector

| # | Problem | Dolor | Tiempo | Dinero |
|---|---------|-------|--------|--------|
| 1 | **Graduate outcomes visible and mediocre.** Ponte en Carrera publishes salary and employment data by program. Prospective students and parents can compare universities directly. | Critical | Low | Critical |
| 2 | **Employability is #1 enrollment driver. Enrollment is revenue.** If outcome data looks worse than competitors, enrollment pressure builds. Post-SUNEDU, 94 universities compete for the same student pool. | Critical | Low | Critical |
| 3 | **No differentiation on international placement.** Zero Peruvian universities do systematic international placement. First mover gets a unique marketing claim. | High | Low | High |
| 4 | **Accreditation and ranking pressure.** SINEACE, QS LatAm, Scimago all factor in employability. International placement data would be a ranking advantage. | Medium | Medium | Medium |
| 5 | **Risk aversion around new vendors.** Approving a new, unproven platform from a startup carries career risk if it fails publicly. | High | Low | Medium |

**The deepest pain:** #1 and #2 are existential. Enrollment is revenue. Employability drives enrollment. Outcomes are public. This isn't a nice-to-have; it's competitive survival in a post-SUNEDU market where 51 universities just died.

**The unlock:** #3 is the opportunity. No competitor does this. First university to show international placements on Ponte en Carrera has a weapon nobody else has. The product makes C1 the hero who brought this capability to the institution.

**The blocker:** #5. New vendor, new category, startup with no track record. The pilot structure must de-risk this completely: low cost, outcome-based, "founding partner" status framing, co-marketing.

## C2: Dean / Faculty Director

| # | Problem | Dolor | Tiempo | Dinero |
|---|---------|-------|--------|--------|
| 1 | **Measured on graduate placement but lacks tools.** Depends on career services (U2) which is under-resourced. | High | Medium | Medium |
| 2 | **Can't differentiate their faculty.** Similar programs at competitor universities. No unique outcome story. | Medium | Low | Medium |
| 3 | **Limited budget authority.** Can champion but can't approve unilaterally. Must sell upward to VP Academic/Rector with a compelling case. | Medium | High | Low |

**The deepest pain:** C2 is squeezed. Responsible for outcomes, dependent on under-resourced U2, lacks budget authority. The product serves C2 best as a ready-made proposal they can carry upward: "here is what it does, here is what it costs, here is the risk profile, here is the competitive advantage."

---

## Pain Ranking (who hurts most, top to bottom)

1. **C1 (VP Academic/Rector):** existential pressure. Enrollment = revenue. Outcomes are public. 94 universities competing.
2. **U1 (Student):** life-changing salary delta they don't even know about. Latent pain, but the stakes are the highest of any user.
3. **U2 (Career Services):** accountable without capability. Daily operational pain.
4. **C2 (Dean):** squeezed between accountability and authority. Needs ammunition more than a product.
