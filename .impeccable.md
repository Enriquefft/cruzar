## Design Context

### Users
- **Primary — Student U0 (22-32, LatAm):** Earning USD 480-670/mo locally, believes international remote work is inaccessible. First lands on landing page or receives email. Emotional state: skeptical (too-good-to-be-true), cautious about scams. Needs to feel: "this is real, verified, people like me actually got the job."
- **Primary — VP Academic / Rector (C1):** Anxious about enrollment rankings (public, post-SUNEDU), skeptical of new vendors. Views deck in formal pitch meetings. Needs to feel: "this is serious, institutional, I would sign with them."
- **Secondary — International employer (remote hiring manager, US/EU):** Receives a candidate application (CV + email). Needs to feel: global professionalism, no "emerging market" discount cues.
- **Internal — Founders/Miura (operators):** Uses dashboards, CV templates, email drafts. Needs: clarity, data density, zero decoration.

### Brand Personality
**Three words:** *institutional, accountable, cross-border.*

- **Institutional** — rectors sign contracts with this brand; parents trust it with their kids' careers. Gravitas, not startup-toy.
- **Accountable** — we price on outcomes, we verify placements. The brand respires numbers, receipts, proof.
- **Cross-border** — verb-name "Cruzar" means "to cross." Spanish-rooted but globally legible. The brand moves students from local salary to USD salary; the identity should feel equally at home in a rector's office in Lima and a CTO's inbox in San Francisco.

Emotional goals by surface:
- Landing → *trust + tangible proof* (skeptical students, worried parents)
- Deck → *institutional seriousness + competitive edge* (rectors)
- CV / email → *global-professional, no regional markers* (employers)
- Dashboard → *operator clarity* (internal)

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Anchors:** Stripe docs (restrained, confident), Mercury / Ramp (fintech-grade institutional), Nubank mature (LatAm global without folclor), editorial press (The Economist, MIT Technology Review — data as hero).
- **Anti-references:** generic AI-platform aesthetic (purple/neon gradients), edtech (birretes, globes, hands holding lightbulbs), bootcamp energy (over-saturated colors, stock photos of smiling diverse teams), LatAm flag colors as identity crutch, "disruption" startup posture.
- **Theme:** light-first for trust surfaces (landing, CV, email) — anxious/skeptical users read light UI as safer. Dark available for internal operator dashboards only if warranted by context.
- **Language:** bilingual-native. Every surface must work unchanged in ES and EN. No hardcoded copy in assets. No "translation-feel" in either direction.

### Design Principles
1. **Credibility before cleverness.** If a design choice is impressive but triggers the slightest vendor-risk alarm in a rector, cut it. Serious first, distinctive second — both are achievable.
2. **Numbers as protagonist.** Placement counts, salary deltas, CEFR levels, verified-by tags — data is the strongest proof we have. Let it lead visually, not ornament it.
3. **Cruzar as structure, not symbol.** The "crossing" metaphor lives in layouts (before → after, local → global, grids that progress), not in arrow icons or bridges. The logotype can work alone.
4. **Bilingual by construction.** Every layout tolerates Spanish's longer words and English's tighter ones. No fragile fixed-width labels.
5. **One system, three registers.** Same tokens power student-facing, rector-facing, and employer-facing surfaces — density and tone shift, primitives don't.
