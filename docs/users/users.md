# Users & Clients

## Distinction

- **Users:** use the product directly.
- **Clients:** pay for the product.
- Overlap is possible. A user can also be a client. A client may never touch the product.

---

## Users

### U1: The Student

Latin American college student or recent grad. Wants better career outcomes, may or may not have "international career" as a conscious goal yet. Earns USD 480-670/month locally at entry level. International remote roles pay 3-5x that (USD 1,500-4,500/month depending on role). English level varies; most have some but aren't confident enough for a professional setting. Has never navigated an international hiring process. Doesn't know what roles exist, what gaps they have, or that a 3-5x salary path is accessible.

**Relationship to product:** direct subject. Is diagnosed, trained, prepared, placed.

### U2: Career Services / Employability Office

Small team (3-8 staff at private universities, 1-3 at public). Runs a "bolsa de trabajo" (job board portal), annual job fairs, CV workshops, company partnerships for domestic internships. Technology is basic: filtered job listing pages, no matching or tracking systems. **International placement is not a function they perform or have ever attempted.** No standardized KPIs or outcome tracking mandated by SUNEDU. Under-resourced relative to the scope of what they're nominally responsible for.

**Relationship to product:** potential operational user, but the product introduces a capability that doesn't exist in their world. They are not upgrading from a worse version of this; they have nothing. Could be a champion if empowered, or a blocker if threatened.

### U3: The Employer (future, not Shape 1)

Global company hiring remote. Receives candidates, evaluates them. Not in scope for Shape 1.

**Relationship to product:** candidate consumer. Future.

## Clients

### C1: VP Academic / Rector (decision maker)

At private universities, budget authority for new initiatives sits at VP Academic or Rector level. Board/promotora sign-off required above modest thresholds. Cares about: enrollment numbers (revenue), institutional reputation, competitive differentiation against ~93 other licensed universities, SINEACE accreditation metrics, and what Ponte en Carrera (government portal) says about their graduate outcomes. **Employability is the #1 factor driving student enrollment decisions.** Ponte en Carrera makes salary and employment data public by university and program, so outcome gaps are visible to every prospective student and parent.

**Relationship to product:** buyer. Decides based on projected outcomes, competitive advantage narrative, risk profile. May never log in. Buys because international placement is a differentiator no Peruvian university currently has.

Note: Previously assumed "dean-level discretionary budget" for the pilot. Research suggests the budget authority sits higher. Pilot pricing may need to fit VP Academic discretionary threshold, or the dean champions it upward with help.

### C2: Dean / Faculty Director (internal champion, potentially)

Controls faculty-level budget but major initiatives require Rector/VP Academic approval at private universities. At public universities, requires Rector + University Council. May be the internal advocate who carries the proposal upward, but is not the final decision maker for a new vendor/platform purchase.

**Relationship to product:** champion who sells upward. Needs ammunition: dashboard previews, outcome projections, competitive framing vs. peer universities.

### C3: The Student (potentially, long-term)

If candidate-pay enters the business model. Not Shape 1.

---

## Key Dynamics

- U2 (career services) has 3-8 people doing job fairs and maintaining a job board. The product gives them a capability they've never had. The question is whether they see it as empowerment or threat.
- C1 (VP Academic/Rector) cares about enrollment, which is driven by employability (#1 factor). Ponte en Carrera makes outcomes public. International placement is a weapon no competitor has.
- C2 (dean) is likely the entry point for the conversation but not the final signer. The pilot proposal needs to be constructed so the dean can carry it upward.
- The 51-university SUNEDU closure displaced 300K+ students into 94 surviving universities. Differentiation pressure is real and structural.
- U1 (student) doesn't know the 3-5x salary path exists. The product's first job is making the opportunity visible before making it accessible.
