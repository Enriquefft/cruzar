# Research: University Enrollment Dynamics & Competitive Pressure in Peru

**Date:** 2026-04-13
**Sources:** SUNEDU annual reports, MINEDU ESCALE, INEI education surveys, Ponte en Carrera, QS Latin America, IPSOS Peru, Grade/FORGE studies

---

## Enrollment Trends

- Pre-pandemic (2019): ~1.8M university students (undergrad + grad).
- COVID dip 2020-2021: estimated 10-15% drop in new enrollments.
- Recovery 2022+: returned to or slightly exceeded pre-pandemic levels by 2024-2025.
- Growth: modest ~2-3% annually, driven by demographic momentum (large 18-22 cohort) + expanded online/hybrid.
- Private universities: ~75% of total enrollment.

## SUNEDU Licensing Shock

Ley Universitaria 30220 (2014) required all universities to meet minimum quality conditions.
- **51 universities denied licenses** and ordered to cease operations (out of ~145 applicants).
- **300,000+ students displaced** (forced to transfer or lost enrollment).
- **~94 universities remain licensed** (public + private).
- Largest structural shock to Peruvian higher education in decades.
- Political backlash (2022-2023 congressional bills to weaken SUNEDU) largely failed. SUNEDU authority holds as of 2026.

## Competitive Metrics

Rankings that matter locally:
- QS Latin America
- Times Higher Education
- SIR (Scimago)
- Top competitors: PUCP, UdL, UTEC, UPC, Universidad del Pacifico. San Marcos leads public.

Other differentiators:
- SUNEDU license status (non-negotiable filter, not differentiator).
- SINEACE accreditation (voluntary but increasingly used in marketing).
- **Ponte en Carrera** (ponteencarrera.pe): government portal publishing graduate employment rates and median salaries by university and program. Forces transparency. Universities can't hide mediocre outcomes.

## Graduate Outcomes Pressure

Competitive pressure on outcomes is **real and increasing**. Ponte en Carrera made data public. Top private universities (UTEC, UPC, UP, PUCP) actively market employability and salary premiums. This is a meaningful enrollment driver.

## Decision-Making Structure

**Public universities:**
- Rector leads, University Assembly + University Council.
- Budgets from national treasury (MINEDU allocation).
- Deans control faculty-level budgets. Major initiatives require Rector + Council.

**Private universities:**
- Governance varies. Many run by boards (promotora/proprietor entities).
- Rector is operationally powerful. Board/promotora controls capital allocation.
- Budget authority for new initiatives: VP Academic or Rector level.
- Board sign-off required for anything above modest thresholds.

## Student/Parent Decision Factors (ranked)

1. **Employability / job placement** (consistently #1)
2. Prestige / brand
3. Cost / financial aid
4. SUNEDU license status (non-negotiable filter)
5. Location
6. Specific program reputation

## Key Takeaways

1. 51 university closures mean 94 survivors compete harder for the same pool. Differentiation pressure is high.
2. Ponte en Carrera forces outcome transparency. International placement (currently zero) would be a massive differentiator visible to every prospective student.
3. Employability is the #1 factor driving enrollment. A university that can show international placements has a marketing weapon.
4. Budget authority sits higher than dean level at private universities. Pilot pricing may need to fit VP Academic discretionary threshold, not dean.
