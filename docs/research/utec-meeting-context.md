## Contact

The meeting is with the head of Career Services at UTEC.

## Role Analysis

This is U2 in our user framework. Operational user, not the budget holder.

At private Peruvian universities, budget authority for new vendor/platform purchases sits at VP Academic or Rector level. Career services cannot unilaterally approve a pilot purchase.

## Meeting Objective (revised)

The meeting is NOT a close. It's an enablement session. The career services lead must leave with:

1. **Conviction:** this solves their daily pain (no tools, no international placement capability, accountable for outcomes they can't produce)
2. **Internal proposal:** a ready-made package they can carry to VP Academic/Rector without having to re-derive or re-explain
3. **Material:** leave-behind artifacts (proposal PDF, one-pager, dashboard mockup) that work without the founder in the room

## Pitch Sequence

Speak to U2's pain first (their operational reality), then arm them with C1's language (enrollment, differentiation, Ponte en Carrera outcomes, competitive positioning).

The career services lead needs to feel two things:
- "This solves MY problem" (personal conviction)
- "I know exactly how to sell this upward" (internal advocacy toolkit)

## Implication for MSP

The demo and sales artifacts must be constructed for TWO audiences:
- Primary (in the room): career services lead. Operational, sees the product working.
- Secondary (not in the room): VP Academic/Rector. Strategic, sees the competitive advantage and numbers.
