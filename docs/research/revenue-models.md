# Research: Revenue Models Across Adjacent Categories

**Date:** 2026-04-13
**Sources:** Company websites, public pricing pages, Deel/Terminal/Turing salary guides, industry reports

---

## Category 1: LatAm-to-US Talent Placement

| Company | Model | Who Pays | Price Points |
|---------|-------|----------|-------------|
| Toptal | Markup on hourly rate | Employer | 40-100% markup; devs billed $60-200+/hr |
| Turing | Markup on salary | Employer | 30-50% markup on contractor rate |
| Andela | Markup/monthly fee | Employer | ~$4K-$6K/mo per dev |
| Terminal | Monthly per-seat + markup | Employer | ~$3K-$5K/mo platform fee + salary |
| Near | One-time placement fee | Employer | ~15-20% of first-year salary |
| Revelo | Placement fee or markup | Employer | ~15-20% annual salary (direct hire) or hourly markup |
| Athyna | Flat placement fee | Employer | ~$5K-$7.5K flat per hire |
| Torre | Freemium + placement fee | Employer | Free tier; paid plans ~$300-500/mo for recruiters |

**Pattern:** Employer always pays. No outcomes-based pricing. Margins from markup (contract) or one-time placement fees (15-20% of salary for direct hire).

## Category 2: Education-to-Employment / University Sales

| Company | Model | Who Pays | Price Points |
|---------|-------|----------|-------------|
| Handshake | Per-institution SaaS (tiered) | University + employers | University: ~$20K-$80K/yr. Employers: ~$30K-$100K+/yr for premium |
| Symplicity | Per-institution SaaS | University | ~$15K-$50K+/yr |
| 12Twenty | Per-institution SaaS | University (mainly B-schools) | ~$20K-$60K/yr |
| GradLeaders | Per-institution SaaS | University | Similar to Symplicity |
| Graduway | Per-institution SaaS | University (alumni offices) | ~$10K-$40K/yr |
| uConnect | Per-institution SaaS | University career centers | ~$10K-$30K/yr |
| Pathrise | Income share / deferred | Candidate | ~$0 upfront; ISA 5-9% of salary for 12-24 months |
| Laboratoria | Hybrid | Employer + grants | Employers ~$3K-$5K per hire; programs often grant-funded |
| Henry | ISA | Candidate | ~15-17% of income for 24 months (capped) |
| Microverse | ISA | Candidate | Was ~15% for 5 years (capped ~$15K); paused/restructured |

**Pattern:** Career services platforms charge universities flat annual SaaS ($15K-$80K). Bootcamp/ISA models charge candidates post-placement. Outcomes-based pricing exists only in ISA models (candidate-funded), not in university SaaS contracts. Handshake is the only two-sided model.

## Category 3: AI Career/Recruiting Agents

| Company | Model | Who Pays | Price Points |
|---------|-------|----------|-------------|
| Mercor | Markup on contractor rate | Employer | ~30-50% markup |
| Micro1 | Markup / placement fee | Employer | Similar to Turing model |
| Sonara | Candidate SaaS subscription | Candidate | ~$25-$50/mo |
| Simplify | Freemium candidate tool | Candidate (free tier); employer job slots | Free auto-apply |
| Final Round AI | Candidate SaaS subscription | Candidate | ~$50-$150/mo |
| Apriora | Per-interview or SaaS | Employer | ~$15-$50 per AI interview or monthly plans |

**Pattern:** Employer-facing AI agents use markup/placement fees. Candidate-facing tools use subscriptions ($25-$150/mo). No outcomes-based pricing.

## Key Finding

No one combines university-as-customer with outcomes-based placement fees. University SaaS is flat annual. Placement economics are always employer-funded. The $8K anchor + outcomes model is genuinely novel: de-risks the university buy while capturing placement upside that normally flows to staffing firms.

## Revenue Model Options for Shape 1

**Option A: Anchor + Outcomes (current pilot)**
- $8K anchor (non-refundable) + up to $12K across 3 outcome milestones
- Pro: de-risks for buyer, aligns incentives, novel
- Con: hard to scale (each deal is bespoke), outcomes take time to realize

**Option B: Per-Student SaaS**
- $200-$500/student/year to the university
- 40 students = $8K-$20K/year (aligns with pilot range)
- Pro: predictable, scales linearly
- Con: no outcome alignment, looks like a software purchase not a placement service

**Option C: Placement Fee (from employer, long-term)**
- 10-15% of first-year salary per placed candidate
- At $30K avg placement: $3K-$4.5K per placement
- At $60K avg placement: $6K-$9K per placement
- Pro: aligns with placement value, scales with comp
- Con: requires employer relationship (not Shape 1), longer sales cycle

**Option D: Hybrid (recommended long-term)**
- University pays anchor/SaaS for the platform (diagnostic + dashboard + preparation)
- Company collects placement fee from employer side when Shape 2/3 activates
- Two revenue streams from two sides of the market
- Pro: builds toward Handshake-like two-sided model at much higher margins
- Con: complexity

For the UTEC pilot: Option A (anchor + outcomes) is correct. Novel, de-risked, aligned. The long-term model is likely Option D but that's a Shape 2+ question.
