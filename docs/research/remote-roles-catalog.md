## English Level Gating

**B2 sufficient:** Dev roles, QA, data analyst, UI/UX, customer support, VA, bookkeeping, project coordination. Deliverables speak louder than conversation.

**C1 unlocks:** SDR/BDR, technical writing, content/copy, client-facing consulting, product management. Conversation-heavy or prose-dependent.

**C2/native required:** Enterprise sales (AE), PR/communications, legal ops.

## Competitive Advantages for LatAm Candidates

1. **Time zone overlap** — single biggest advantage over Asian remote talent. US companies pay premium for real-time collaboration.
2. **Portfolio/GitHub over credentials** — demonstrable projects outweigh degrees for tech roles.
3. **High-value certifications:** AWS/GCP (DevOps), HubSpot (marketing/SDR), Google Analytics, QuickBooks (bookkeeping), Scrum/PMP (project coord).
4. **Async communication skills** — written Slack/Loom fluency matters as much as spoken English.
5. **Cultural proximity** — familiarity with US business norms.

## Sweet Spot for First Cohort

**$18-35K/year** (junior tech, senior non-tech). High enough to be life-changing in Peru (3-5x local salary). Low enough that companies actively prefer LatAm over US hires. This IS the bottom of the "abandoned middle" the thesis targets. First placements enter here; the system moves candidates up over time.

## Role Catalog Implications

For the MSP diagnostic, the role catalog should include:
- 3-4 technical roles (frontend, backend, QA, data analyst)
- 3-4 non-technical roles (customer support, VA, SDR, marketing)
- Spanning $10K-55K to cover the realistic range
- Each role with: compensation, required English level, key skills, typical employer

Non-technical roles are important: a university cohort is mostly NOT software engineers. Business, engineering (non-CS), and social sciences majors need viable matches too.
