## Crowded Positioning Language

- "Hire [adjective] talent from [geography]" (Near, Toptal, Andela, Terminal)
- "AI recruiter/agent for employers" (Mercor, Apriora, micro1)
- "The future of work" (generic, claimed by many)
- "Top 3% / world-class / outstanding" (quality superlatives are noise)
- "Talent marketplace" (Torre, generic LatAm plays)

## Unclaimed Whitespace

1. **University as the customer.** Every company sells to employers or individual candidates. Nobody positions the institution as the buyer. Genuinely empty quadrant.

2. **Diagnosis-to-placement pipeline.** Education companies stop at "we teach skills." Staffing companies start at "we have pre-vetted talent." Nobody owns the full arc from assessment through preparation through placement as a single system.

3. **"AI infrastructure for institutions" framing.** All "AI platform" claims are employer-facing. Nobody says "AI infrastructure that a university plugs into."

4. **Outcome accountability to institutions.** ISA/outcome models exist with students (Henry, Microverse). Nobody offers outcome-based pricing to universities.

5. **Voice/interview training as institutional product.** Final Round AI helps candidates cheat live interviews. Nobody positions interview preparation as institutional curriculum.

## Positioning Recommendation

**Avoid:** "talent marketplace," "hire from LatAm," "the future of work," "top N%," "AI recruiting platform"

**Lean into:** the intersection of B2B-institutional buyer + full-pipeline AI + emerging-market origin. That intersection is empty.

The "AI-native search firm" framing from the handoff is close but not quite right:
- "Search firm" implies employer-side (the employer hires the search firm)
- This company is supply-side (the institution/candidate is the customer)
- Better framing: the company is an **AI-native placement engine** that institutions plug into, or **institutional talent infrastructure**

The one-sentence should answer: "what does the university get?" not "what category are you in?"
