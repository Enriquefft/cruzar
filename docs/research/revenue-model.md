## Revenue Model Options

### Per-student SaaS license ($50-$200/student/year)
- Pros: Predictable, easy to budget, scales with enrollment
- Cons: Disconnected from outcomes, commoditizes toward $1/student floor
- Signals: "We are a software company"

### Per-cohort flat fee ($5K-$25K per cohort)
- Pros: Simple, predictable, natural pilot structure
- Cons: No outcome alignment, resistance if first cohort underperforms
- Signals: "We are a program provider"

### Per-placement success fee (15-30% of first-year comp or $1K-$5K flat)
- Pros: Zero risk for university, extreme outcome alignment, high revenue per placement
- Cons: Delayed/unpredictable cash flow, requires tracking/verification
- Signals: "We are a recruiting firm"

### Hybrid (platform fee + success fee) -- RECOMMENDED
- Pros: Base covers operating costs + signals university commitment, success fee captures upside, mirrors exec search retainer + completion
- Cons: Slightly complex to explain
- Signals: "We are a platform that delivers outcomes"
- **This is what the $8K anchor + $12K outcomes pilot uses.**

### Outcomes-based with milestones
- Pros: Most granular alignment, university sees value accruing before placement
- Cons: Most complex, milestone definitions become negotiation points
- Signals: "We are accountable at every step"

## Pilot Pricing Assessment

The $8K anchor + up to $12K outcomes for 40 students over 12 weeks is well-calibrated:
- $8K base is below the Handshake US benchmark (~$8K/year), signaling affordability for LatAm
- $300/student if all 40 place is 0.5% of first-year comp at $60K, far below exec search 25-35%
- $20K ceiling is within pilot budget range for mid-to-large Peruvian private universities ($10K-$30K realistic for new initiatives)
- Risk for university limited to $8K if outcomes disappoint
- **Pricing is conservative.** Significant room to raise success fees in future cohorts.

One concern from research: budget authority for this amount may sit at VP Academic/Rector, not dean. Confirm with UTEC contact.

## Long-Term Revenue Model

### Multi-sided platform evolution
- Year 1: University pays (pilots). Students free.
- Year 2-3: Employers pay placement fees (15-25% of first-year comp). This is where real revenue lives. A single $80K placement at 20% = $16K.
- Year 3+: Optional candidate premium services. Keep small to maintain supply-side network effects.

### Path from $20K pilots to $1M+ ARR
- Year 1: 3-5 university pilots at ~$20K = $60-100K. Purpose: prove outcomes, build case studies.
- Year 2: Convert pilots to annual ($30-50K), add 5-10 universities, begin employer revenue. Target: $300-500K.
- Year 3: 20+ universities, employer placement revenue scaling. Target: $1M+.

### Inflection point
When enough placed alumni exist that employers come directly (pull > push). University contracts become channel cost. Employer fees become primary revenue engine.

### Mercor as valuation comp
$75M ARR at $2B valuation (Feb 2025) with 30% employer fees. Validates the revenue model for AI-native recruiting at scale.
